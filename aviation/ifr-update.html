IFR Update - June 2024 (WIP)

Idle Hands are the Devil's Workshop

I've completed my first listen of IFR ground school, and I now have a few IFR flights under my belt. I did a couple of flights in a six pack aircraft, and another couple in a G1000.

No matter how much I tried to like the six pack over the G1000, I can't deny that the G1000 simply offers much stronger situational awareness, and my flying is more precise.

What I've Learned So Far

Known Power Settings

The first and most important thing I've done to improve my instrument flying is to print the following table Power and Pitch for Desired State for a Cessna 172. Sourced from MidwestFlyer.com and put it on my aircraft's yoke mount. For any given state in IFR flight, it gives me quick pitch and power setting I can use to approximate the state I need.

By using known power settings, I'm able to shift my focus to managing the aircraft and interfacing with the IFR system instead of trying to get my trim just right. I'm still pretty low hour in C172's, so this may be less valuable for pilots who did their PPL training in the same aircraft they're learning IFR.

WIRE

WIRE is an acronym to guide pilots when preparing for an upcoming IFR approach. It should be completed as early as is reasonably possible. It stands for

  • W: Weather I can't always receive weather when I'm starting my WIRE briefing. If that's the case, I just do weather at the end (IREW).
  • I: Instruments
  • R: Radios
  • E: En-Route Briefing

G1000 Reference Altitudes

For Non-Precision Approaches, you may not descend below the Minimum Descent Alittude, but on Precision Approaches you may descend below the Decision Altitude briefly to assess if a landing can be made. § 91.175(c) Takeoff and landing under IFR states that "no pilot may ... continue an approach below the authorized DA/DH unless" the pilot has determined that a landing can be safely achieved.

Given this, it makes sense to set different values in the G1000's reference alittude for the appraoches. For a non-precision approach, I was instructed to set the refernce altitude 50 feet above the actual MDA. For a precision approach, I program the DA as the reference altitude.
